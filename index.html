The Biomaking Space

Where UIUC Students Develop Solutions for the Planet

Join Learn More

The Biomaking Space is based out of the Urbana-Champaign Community Fab Lab, which is a place where anyone in the local community can learn how to make everything from textiles to electronics to 3D printed prototypes. It is one of many makerspaces that exist across the globe.

Anyone can make a positive impact in the world — which is why we need more places where people of all backgrounds can foster their creativity and learn the skills that will help them shape the future.

While the Fab Lab in general is a great resource, the Biomaking Space is the only place on campus where any student — regardless of their major or prior experience — can harness advanced technologies like biotech and renewable energy to address the world’s most pressing environmental problems. It is one model of a community biology lab. Meaning, it has all of the basic equipment and procedures that are expected of a biosafety level 1 biology lab that deals with non-pathogenic organisms — but gives students more freedom to explore their own interests.

Using the tools of makerspaces and community biology labs, the Biomaking Space can support a community of students working on important challenges like climate change, water pollution, and loss of biodiversity. Some of the student efforts include:

CreAlgae

This is a project within Illinois Enactus, a social entrepreneurship RSO, that is creating bioplastic products from harmful algae blooms while positively impacting local wildlife.

Wood from Bacteria

One of the biggest drivers of the deforestation — which is a major contributor of climate change — are wood products. So to stop some of the destruction, this project is developing alternative wood with bacteria.

Rainforest Xprize

Xprize is a non-profit that organizes multi-million dollar, global competitions around some of the world’s most pressing challenges. Recently, it launched one for rainforest species discovery, and there is interest in registering a U of I team.
